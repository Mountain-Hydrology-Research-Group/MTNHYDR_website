Sublimation of Snow

Project Background and Motivation:

Winter snowpack in the mountains of a river basin is often used to inform how much water is expected to be available throughout the year, However, there has been an unexpected mismatch between the winter snowpack totals in the headwaters of the Colorado and resultant discharge coming down the Colorado River in recent years. For example, in both 2020 and 2021, snowpack totals within the upper Colorado basin were at or above normal, but streamflows within the basin were around 30 to 50% of there historic normal values. Where did the water go? One of the major uncertainties within the water balance is snow sublimation.
